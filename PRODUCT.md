# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences read this portfolio; both must be served without one undermining the other.

**Recruiter / HR (fast scan)**: Lands from a job application or LinkedIn. Has 90 seconds. Needs: who is this person, what is their stack, what have they built, where to reach them. Leaves without scrolling if the hero doesn't orient them.

**Tech lead / senior dev (depth read)**: Evaluates technical judgment. Reads project descriptions, checks architecture decisions, looks for curiosity. Wants signal that Tom thinks carefully, not just ships things.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Tom Bertomeu, fullstack developer (3rd-year CS BUT, alternance). Exists to convert readers into contacts — a message sent, a CV downloaded, an interview requested. Secondary: positions Tom as someone who takes craft seriously, not just a junior who built a portfolio because the bootcamp said to.

## Brand Personality

Confident · Technical · Clean

Voice is direct, not informal. Self-aware without being self-deprecating. The work speaks; the copy doesn't oversell it. A recruiter should feel "this person knows what they're doing." A senior dev should feel "this person cares about the details."

## Anti-references

- Overenthusiastic junior dev portfolios: "Hi! I'm passionate about building amazing things that make a difference!" — hollow enthusiasm, no specificity.
- Template-flavored portfolios with identical card grids, uppercase eyebrows on every section, and hero backgrounds from a Framer/Notion template library.
- The "skills list with progress bars" pattern — communicates nothing, reads as filler.
- The single-page scroll with no personality: white background, Inter font, blue accent, three project cards, contact form. Seen 10,000 times.

## Design Principles

1. **First impression earns the scroll.** The hero must orient — who, what, why engage — before asking for any effort from the visitor. Placeholders are broken products.
2. **Confidence is specific.** Vague enthusiasm ("passionate developer") signals nothing. Specific claims ("built X for Y, reduced Z by N%") signal capability.
3. **Technical identity, not technical costume.** The design uses technical motifs (grid, monospace, precision) because Tom IS technical, not because developer portfolios "look like this."
4. **Dual-reader parity.** Neither audience is an afterthought. The fast scan path (hero → about → CV download) and the depth read path (experience → projects → contact) must both be complete and satisfying.
5. **Craft is the proof.** Every interaction detail — hover, transition, focus state, error message — signals the same care Tom puts into his code. Sloppy UI is a broken argument.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Both FR/EN audiences. Reduced motion respected (already implemented via `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`). Contrast minimum 4.5:1 for all body text.
